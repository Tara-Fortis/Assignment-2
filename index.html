Tress of the Emerald Sea

Brandon Sanderson is a major figure in contemporary fantasy, known for building the Cosmere, a shared universe that links series such as Mistborn and The Stormlight Archive. His approach emphasizes clear rules for magic, treating it almost like a science, which has influenced how readers and writers think about the genre.

In 2023, he released Tress of the Emerald Sea as the first of four “Secret Projects” written during the pandemic. Inspired by the playful tone of The Princess Bride, the novel shows how Sanderson can shift style while keeping the structural clarity and interconnected design that define his work.

Synopsis

The only life Tress has known on her island home in an emerald-green ocean has been a simple one, with the simple pleasures of collecting cups brought by sailors from faraway lands and listening to stories told by her friend Charlie. But when his father takes him on a voyage to find a bride and disaster strikes, Tress must stow away on a ship and seek the Sorceress of the deadly Midnight Sea. Amid the spore oceans where pirates abound, can Tress leave her simple life behind and make her own place sailing a sea where a single drop of water can mean instant death?

[SPOILERS] Mild Concept Spoilers Ahead

Guys, I just finished Tress, and we need to talk about the Spore Seas. Seriously, this might be some of the most original worldbuilding Brandon has ever done, and this is from the guy who invented allomancy and surgebinding.

This book feels like he took the worldbuilding of a massive epic, compressed it into one adventure, and gave us a really fresh fantasy setting with incredibly high stakes derived entirely from the planetary ecology. It's the kind of worldbuilding you want to immediately start charting on a whiteboard. If you are a fan of unique magic systems that blend environmental science, check this out!

DragonSteel Entertainment LLC

Dragonsteel Entertainment LLC was founded by Brandon Sanderson in 2021 and holds copyrights to many of Brandon's books. On their website they sell specialty books, art prints, collectibles, apparel, themed merchandise, and seasonal items. The company is guided by its mission statement: “Creating our worlds to inspire yours.”

Dragonsteel specializes in producing exclusive editions of Sanderson’s works, including The Stormlight Archive, Mistborn, and Cytoverse. Beyond books, the company offers a wide range of products such as leatherbound editions, art prints, apparel, themed merchandise like pins and dice sets, and seasonal collectibles including holiday cards and Radiant Order gear.

Community engagement is a central part of Dragonsteel’s identity. The company hosts fan-focused events such as Light Day and participates in conventions like DSNX25. It also maintains a blog that shares updates on Cosmere continuity, behind-the-scenes projects, and themed celebrations, while offering fans immersive experiences through features like “Enter the Cognitive Realm.”

Legally, Dragonsteel Entertainment LLC holds copyright over its products and publications, with all rights reserved under © 2025 Dragonsteel Books. At its core, the company reflects Brandon Sanderson’s vision of blending storytelling with collectible design, offering readers and fans a way to connect deeply with the Cosmere universe.
