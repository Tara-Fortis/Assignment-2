Synopsis

The only life Tress has known on her island home in an emerald-green ocean has been a simple one, with the simple pleasures of collecting cups brought by sailors from faraway lands and listening to stories told by her friend Charlie. But when his father takes him on a voyage to find a bride and disaster strikes, Tress must stow away on a ship and seek the Sorceress of the deadly Midnight Sea. Amid the spore oceans where pirates abound, can Tress leave her simple life behind and make her own place sailing a sea where a single drop of water can mean instant death?

A Cosmere Classic

As someone who has been devouring every page Brandon Sanderson has written since Elantris, Tress of the Emerald Sea isn't just a book, its a GIFT!!! This secret project felt like sailing to a brand new shore

I love Sanderson's books for his intricate epics, and here he proves his mastery of the elevated fairy tale. The different "seas" of spores is a breathtaking application of his hard magic system. Also, having Hoid narrate is just a treat! It adds a layer of wit and heart that long-time Cosmere fans will truely enjoy.

Super_Sanderfan

Tress of the Emerald Sea[WARNING]Mild Concept Spoilers

Guys, I just finished Tres, and we need to talk about the Spore Seas. Seriously, this might be some of the most original worldbuilding Brandon has ever done, and this is from the guy who invented allomancy and surgebinding.

This book feels like he took the worldbuilding of a massive epic, compressed it into one adventure, and gave us a really fresh fantasy setting with incredibly high stakes derived enirely from the planetary ecology. It's the kind of worldbuilding you want to immediately start charting on a whiteboard. If you are a fan of unique magic systems that blend environmental science, check this out!
